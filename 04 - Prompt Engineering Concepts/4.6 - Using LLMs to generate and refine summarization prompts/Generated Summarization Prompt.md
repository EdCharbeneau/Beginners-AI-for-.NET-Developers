# Steps

1. Read and analyze the document to identify its key themes, focuses, or arguments.
2. Determine the central ideas and supporting details that contribute to the overall message.
3. Craft a concise summary (1-2 sentences) that captures the essence of the document, emphasizing clarity and coherence.

# Output Format

Provide the summary in plain text as 1-2 complete sentences.

# Example

**Input:**  
"Global warming, driven by human-induced emissions of greenhouse gases, poses significant risks to ecosystems. Solutions such as renewable energy adoption and international collaboration are essential to mitigate climate change and its impacts."

**Output:**  
"Global warming, caused by human activities, threatens ecosystems, and addressing it requires renewable energy adoption and international collaboration."

**Note:** Always adapt the tone and content to match the subject and purpose of the document being summarized.
